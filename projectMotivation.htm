Why did I choose to build a Blockchain project?

The core reason I chose to develop this project comes from my final year in college, where I took a module called Blockchain Technology. From the beginning, I found the module genuinely interesting and was curious about how everything worked under the hood.

During this module, I was introduced to key blockchain concepts such as:

  • Peer-to-Peer Networks (P2P)
  • Blocks & Transactions
  • Tokens (Fungible & Non-Fungible)
  • Addresses & Keypairs (Public & Private)
  • Governance
  • Decentralized Autonomous Organizations (DAOs)

One topic that stood out to me in particular was DAOs. During a lecture, we were introduced to Nouns DAO and the concept immediately resonated with me. The idea of decentralized decision-making and community-driven governance was something I found especially compelling.

I also explored consensus mechanisms such as:

  • Proof of Work (PoW) – where miners must solve computational problems (e.g. finding a nonce that produces a hash with leading zeros) to validate blocks. This is the model used by Bitcoin.
  • Proof of Stake (PoS) – where validators stake funds (e.g. 32 ETH in Ethereum) to participate in block validation. While I studied PoS, I did not heavily implement it in this project. Ethereum now uses this model.
  • Ethereum Virtual Machine (EVM) – responsible for executing smart contracts on the blockchain.
  • Smart Contracts & Solidity

What really captured my interest was the concept of decentralization. Unlike traditional centralized systems (such as banks), where institutions control transactions and can reverse them, blockchain systems operate without a central authority. This means users have full control over their assets, but also full responsibility.

After being exposed to these ideas, I became highly motivated to explore the technology further. Over the summer following my final year, I decided to build my own blockchain simulation to better understand how these systems work in practice.

What does my project include?

Using the core concepts I learned, I set out to implement a simplified blockchain system from scratch, focusing on simulating real-world behaviour.

  • Block Creation
  • Wallet Creation
  • DAO Functionality:
    • Bidding system to win rewards
    • Bid tracking & history
    • Live reward display
    • Bid before the time is up - highest bid amount wins.
    • Wallet connection (including multiple wallets)
    • Treasury system (aggregating all bids)
    • Dynamic current bid tracking
    Proposals & Candidates (DAO Feature):
    • Only members who have won an Achievement Card in the DAO can create proposals.
    • Anyone can view all submitted proposals.
    • Connected wallets can cast votes for the proposals they wish to support.
    • Everyone can see which Aliases have voted for which proposals.
    • The total votes for each proposal are visible to all. For example, Proposal 1 might have 5 votes For, 1 Against and 2 Abstain. This information is available for every proposal.

Potential Future Enhancements

  • Peer-to-peer (P2P) network.
  • Allow my blockchain to fork (for learning purposes).
  • Block height limits and data management (like Bitcoin).
  • Dashboard for analytics.
  • Mini EVM Support – a compiler or interpreter for a small subset of Solidity (.sol), enabling basic smart contract functionality within the project.
